About the Exam

IIT JAM is conducted every year by the IITs and IISc on a rotational basis (IIT Bombay is the organising institute for JAM 2026). A strong JAM score doesn't just earn you a postgraduate seat — it places you inside India's premier research ecosystems, with access to world-class labs, fellowships and faculty. For science graduates, it is the most direct route from a B.Sc to a research-driven career.

ALLIN's programme blends concept mastery (the K in KICN) with intensive problem practice, mentor-led doubt solving and full-length tests modelled on the real Computer-Based Test. You study through live and recorded classes you can revisit anytime, so revision is always on demand.

Who Is It For?

  • B.Sc students in Physics, Chemistry or Life Sciences targeting a top M.Sc
  • Final-year graduates who want a research or academic career
  • Aspirants aiming for integrated Ph.D. and fellowship pathways at IISc & IITs

Syllabus Breakdown

  • Mathematical Methods & Mechanics
  • Electricity & Magnetism, Electromagnetic Theory
  • Oscillations, Waves & Optics
  • Kinetic Theory & Thermodynamics
  • Modern Physics & Solid State; Electronics & Devices
  • Physical Chemistry — thermodynamics, kinetics, electrochemistry, quantum
  • Organic Chemistry — mechanisms, stereochemistry, named reactions
  • Inorganic Chemistry — periodicity, coordination, main-group & transition
  • Spectroscopy & analytical fundamentals
  • Cell Biology, Genetics & Molecular Biology
  • Biochemistry & Microbiology
  • Plant & Animal Physiology; Ecology & Evolution
  • Foundations of Biotechnology (BT also tests Physics, Chemistry & Maths basics)
  • 3-hour Computer-Based Test (CBT)
  • Three sections: MCQ, MSQ (multiple select) and NAT (numerical answer)
  • Negative marking on MCQs only — accuracy strategy taught
  • Full-length mocks with detailed performance analysis
